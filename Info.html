Our History

The Indian National Parks Conservation Organization was established in [First:1936] with a vision to protect and preserve the rich biodiversity and natural heritage of India's national parks. Recognizing the importance of these ecosystems, our organization has been actively involved in conservation efforts for several decades.

Over the years, we have worked tirelessly to address environmental challenges, promote sustainable practices, and raise awareness about the significance of national parks in maintaining ecological balance.

Benefits of Conservation

Conservation of national parks brings forth a myriad of benefits for both humans and nature. It ensures the protection of diverse plant and animal species, contributes to scientific research, and promotes environmental awareness. Some key benefits include:

  • Preservation of Biodiversity
  • Ecosystem Stability
  • Scientific Research Opportunities
  • Education and Awareness
  • Economic Benefits through Tourism
  • Climate Regulation
  • Water Resource Protection
  • Cultural and Aesthetic Value

Types of National Parks

Wildlife Sanctuaries

Home to diverse wildlife, these sanctuaries provide a protected environment for animals to thrive. Visitors can witness these creatures in their natural habitat.

Biosphere Reserves

These reserves integrate conservation with sustainable development. They aim to reconcile the conservation of biodiversity with its sustainable use.

Heritage National Parks

These parks conserve areas with cultural or historical significance, protecting both natural and cultural treasures for future generations.

Marine National Parks

Located in coastal areas, these parks aim to protect marine ecosystems, including coral reefs and marine life, fostering sustainable marine conservation.

Mountain National Parks

Nestled in mountainous regions, these parks contribute to the preservation of unique mountain ecosystems, protecting rare flora and fauna.

Explore the diverse types of national parks, each playing a crucial role in preserving our natural and cultural heritage.

Our Purpose

The primary purpose of our organization is to contribute to the conservation and sustainable management of national parks in India. We aim to:

  • Preserve and protect the unique ecosystems within national parks.
  • Facilitate research initiatives to better understand and safeguard biodiversity.
  • Educate and engage the public in environmental awareness and conservation efforts.
  • Collaborate with government bodies, NGOs, and local communities to implement effective conservation strategies.
